Surviving from Wild Fire - Guidelines

Before the Wild Fire:

  • Create a defensible space around your property by clearing vegetation and debris.
  • Have an emergency evacuation plan and communicate it with your family members.
  • Keep your important documents and valuables in a fireproof safe or off-site storage.
  • Stay informed about wildfire risks in your area and follow local authorities' instructions.

During the Wild Fire:

  • Listen to emergency broadcasts and evacuation orders. Follow the recommended routes.
  • If trapped, seek shelter in a cleared area with minimal vegetation and stay low to the ground.
  • Keep windows and doors closed to prevent smoke from entering your home.
  • Do not use elevators and ensure that your escape routes are clear.

After the Wild Fire:

  • Wait for authorities to declare it safe before returning to your home or affected area.
  • Inspect your property for any signs of damage or hazards before entering.
  • Contact your insurance company to report any losses or damages caused by the wildfire.
  • Take steps to prevent post-fire hazards like erosion and potential mudslides.
